Luis E. Sánchez-González

Master's student • B.Sc. in Physics

Deparment of Physics
Faculty of Science | U.A.B.C
Ensenada, B.C., MX

I’m master's student in Prof. Ramón Carrillo-Bastos’s theory group, part of the Quantum Transport and Optical Phenomena Research Group at the Faculty of Science, UABC.

My research focuses on theoretical condensed matter physics, particularly on the electronic, optical, and topological properties of two-dimensional quantum materials. I combine analytical and numerical techniques to study systems such as graphene, Dirac materials and topological insulators.

Alongside my academic work, I have strong technical skills in Python and C/C++, with experience in scientific computing and data analysis. I'm constantly learning new tools and enjoy sharing knowledge through teaching, mentoring, and science communication.

I'm currently preparing for a PhD in physics and also developing skills relevant to data science and AI, aiming to contribute in academia and industry.
